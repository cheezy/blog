I was in a conversation with a potential new client last Friday, and they told me that they still had some of their development outsourced to an offshore company. After the call I started to think about the outsourcing model. How will it be impacted by AI? It led me to a conclusion that might be uncomfortable for some.

### Why did companies outsource?

In my experience, there have been two primary reasons for companies to outsource development:

1. Cost - the ability to hire developers at a lower cost than they could find in the local market.
2. Availability - the ability to find developers with the skills they need. This is especially true in smaller markets.

I am sure there are other reasons, but these are the two I have seen and what I will focus on in this post.

### Do these reasons still hold true in the age of AI?

_Could an offshore developer or tester be replaced by an AI Agent?_

This is the question that has to be answered and it is not as straightforward as it may seem. In many environments today, Agents still need to be monitored by a human to clarify requirements and to provide permissions to perform some actions. This is becoming less of an issue with each advance in the AI models, but it still exists at this time. We know that a Developer currently equipped with the latest tools and a robust configuration can produce many times the amount of code with higher quality than they could without AI.

It is also becoming much easier for a Developer to run more than one Agent at a time. This takes the productivity gains described in the previous paragraph and scales them up. Add to this any one of the robust tools that exist to coordinate work between multiple Agents and you have a very strong delivery team with just one or two Developers.

In my opinion, the cost and availability of people arguments for outsourcing are no longer valid. It is now more cost effective to have a small team working together with AI to deliver software than to outsource to an offshore company.
